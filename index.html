What is Arduino Day?

Arduino Day is a worldwide birthday celebration of Arduino. It's a 24-hour-long event—organized directly by the community, or by the Arduino team—that brings people together to share their experiences and learn more about the open-source platform.

Code with MakerGuru

A Hands-On Workshop & Live Demo Experience @ Maker Bhavan, IIT Gandhinagar

10:00 AM

Welcome & Arduino Day Introduction

  • What is Arduino Day?
  • What is Open Hardware?
  • Why Arduino changed the world of electronics
  • Introduction to Makerspace IITGN Community

Outcome: Participants understand the global impact of Arduino and open innovation.

10:15 AM

Live Demo 1 – Meet Your First Arduino

  • Introduction to Arduino Board
  • Understanding pins (Digital, Analog, Power)
  • What is a microcontroller?
  • Connecting Arduino to computer & Installing Arduino IDE
Live Demo: Uploading the “Blink” Program. Participants see their first LED blink — the iconic Arduino moment.

Outcome: Participants understand how code interacts with hardware.

10:45 AM

Hands-On Coding Session – Code Your First Project

Participants (individually or in small teams):

  • Write and upload LED blink code
  • Modify delay timing
  • Add push button input & Create simple ON/OFF logic
Challenge Task: “Can you control the LED using a button?”

Outcome: Understand digital I/O, learn basic C/C++ structure, and build confidence in debugging.

11:30 AM

Break & Open Interaction

  • Informal discussion
  • Display of Arduino-based projects
  • Q&A with MakerGuru mentors
11:45 AM

Live Demo 2 – Sensors & Intelligence

Demonstration of Ultrasonic, Temperature, and Light sensors, plus a Servo motor.

Live Example: Distance-based obstacle detection & Automatic light control system.

Outcome: Participants understand how sensors make systems “smart”.

12:15 PM

Mini Build Challenge – Code to Solve

Participants will build a line follower robot using an Arduino board.

Outcome: Apply coding knowledge, experience rapid prototyping, and strengthen problem-solving.

12:45 PM

Showcase & Closing

  • 2–3 teams demonstrate their working setup
  • Quick reflections: “What did you build?”
  • Introduction to advanced pathways (IoT, Robotics, AI integration)

“You didn’t just learn Arduino today — you powered your first innovation.”

Learning Outcomes

By the end of “Code with MakerGuru”, participants will:

  • Understand fundamentals of Arduino and microcontrollers
  • Write and modify simple Arduino programs
  • Interface sensors and actuators
  • Learn debugging basics
  • Gain exposure to open hardware ecosystem
  • Develop confidence in hardware prototyping
  • Take first step toward embedded systems & IoT
